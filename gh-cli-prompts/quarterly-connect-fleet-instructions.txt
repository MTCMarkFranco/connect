QUARTERLY CONNECT FLEET INSTRUCTION PACK

PURPOSE
Use GitHub Copilot CLI with /fleet to produce a full quarterly Connect draft that is strategic, evidence-backed, and aligned to the structure and tone of the past sample Connect. The workflow must combine user-supplied quarter information, user-supplied core metrics, and evidence gathered from WorkIQ across emails, Teams messages, documents, and Loop content.

ROLE CONTEXT
The author is a Pro Code developer focused on the Azure platform. Core expertise spans the full Azure stack including but not limited to: AKS, ARO, AI Search, Microsoft Fabric, Azure SQL Database, Cosmos DB, Redis Cache, App Service, Azure Functions, Integration Services, Web Apps, React front-ends, Azure AI Foundry, Foundry IQ Services, Agentic AI design, Foundry agents and tools, evaluations, models, and AI Landing Zones. Adjacent focus areas include Azure DevOps, GitHub, and GitHub Copilot.

The author's revenue model centres on Azure Consumption Revenue (ACR) and Microsoft Fabric consumption. The author does not sell Copilot licences. Any Copilot references should reflect enablement, developer productivity, or technical advocacy rather than licence sales. Licensing evidence should focus on Azure consumption and Fabric consumption only.

Do not include Copilot Studio wins as standalone achievements. Only reference Copilot Studio if the author built a hybrid solution for a customer that included it as a component (e.g. Quebecor). The focus should remain on the custom pro-code work, not the Copilot Studio element itself.

3. The user supplies the current core metrics for that quarter.
4. Optional but helpful inputs:

   - sensitive topics or customers to avoid naming directly
   - any role or goal updates that should influence future-looking content

NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES
1. Do not invent facts, outcomes, praise, or examples.
2. No meaningful claim may appear in the final Connect unless it is backed by evidence.
3. Every major example must include source metadata: source type, rough date, people or group involved, and why it matters.
4. Prefer evidence from the target quarter. If older evidence is used for context, label it clearly as prior context.
5. Prefer paraphrased evidence over verbatim copying, especially for sensitive or customer-specific content.
6. If evidence is weak, conflicting, or absent, surface a gap instead of filling it with generic language.
7. The final narrative should emphasize strategic business value, customer outcomes, leadership, collaboration, growth, and culture impact.

PARALLEL WORKSTREAMS FOR /FLEET
When possible, split the job into these parallel workstreams:

1. Metrics and strategic storyline
   - Parse the supplied core metrics.
   - Pull out the strongest signals on Azure consumption growth, Fabric consumption, customer impact, solution deployments, demand, satisfaction, journey progression, opportunity creation, and technical breadth across the Azure stack.
   - Convert raw metrics into strategic value statements without overstating causality.
   - Focus on ACR and Fabric consumption as primary revenue indicators. Do not reference Copilot licence sales.

2. Customer impact evidence
   - Search WorkIQ for concrete examples tied to customer value, decision-making, opportunity progression, trusted-advisor moments, follow-up actions, and measurable outcomes.
   - Find examples that show how the work was done, not just what was delivered.
   - Prioritise evidence involving Azure platform services (AKS, ARO, Cosmos DB, Fabric, AI Search, Foundry, App Service, Functions, SQL, Redis, Integration Services, AI Landing Zones, Agentic AI) and developer tooling (Azure DevOps, GitHub, GitHub Copilot).

3. Community and event evidence
   - Search for community contributions, internal enablement, cross-team leadership, external events, hackathons, conferences, and hosted sessions.
   - Capture who benefited and what changed because of the contribution.

4. Recognition, kudos, and coaching evidence
   - Find kudos given to others, coaching delivered, coaching received, and awards or recognition received.
   - Separate giving impact from receiving recognition.

5. Growth and setback evidence
   - Search for moments where plans changed, challenges appeared, or feedback led to a stronger outcome.
   - Identify growth signals that can support the reflection section.

6. Validation and synthesis
   - Merge all evidence.
   - Remove duplicates.
   - Rank evidence strength.
   - Identify unsupported themes.
   - Draft the final Connect only after validation is complete.

EVIDENCE SCHEMA
Every material point should map to one or more records using this schema:

- claim_id: short unique identifier
- theme: one of business-impact, customer-outcome, azure-solution, consumption-revenue, community, event, kudos-given, kudos-received, coaching-delivered, coaching-received, award, setback, growth, future-goal
- section: results, setbacks-growth, future-goals, behaviors, or cross-cutting
- claim_summary: one sentence summary of the point the draft may make
- source_type: email, Teams, document, Loop, metric, or mixed
- source_reference: subject, title, thread label, document name, or equivalent reference
- date_or_period: exact date if known, otherwise rough period inside the quarter
- people_or_orgs: key people, team, customer, or community involved
- concrete_example: the specific action, event, decision, or outcome
- business_or_cultural_value: why this matters strategically
- related_metric: metric text or number if connected to the supplied metrics
- evidence_strength: strong, medium, or weak
- usage_guidance: how this should be used in the final draft
- caveats: missing details, ambiguity, or sensitivity handling notes

EVIDENCE QUALITY BAR
Use these standards during validation:
- Strong: direct evidence from first-hand artifacts, clear date, clear people, and a concrete outcome or example
- Medium: credible evidence with partial metadata or weaker directness
- Weak: vague praise, incomplete context, or no concrete example

Do not use weak evidence for major claims unless it is explicitly labeled as tentative and paired with a follow-up question.

PART 3 - FULL CONNECT DRAFT
Draft a full Connect aligned to the sample structure:

A. Reflect on the past
- What results did you deliver, and how did you do it?

B. Reflect on recent setbacks
- What did you learn, and how did you grow?

C. Plan for the future
- What are your goals for the upcoming period?
- How will your actions and behaviors help you reach your goals?

Drafting guidance:
- Write in first person.
- Sound confident, specific, and thoughtful, not inflated.
- Lead with business and customer outcomes.
- Show how the work reflects Microsoft values, collaboration, leadership, and growth.
- Use concrete examples and metrics naturally in the narrative.
- For future goals, root the goals in the quarter's evidence and supplied priorities. If explicit future priorities were not supplied, propose grounded next-step goals and label them as recommended.
